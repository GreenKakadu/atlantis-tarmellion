Description of ship types of Tarmellion

Raft

An extremely simple ship built out of roughhewn logs that are lashed together to form a rectangular craft with almost no steerage and propulsion.

Rowingboat

The rowingboat is a small ship that is normally used as a deep sea fishing boat or life saving device. A small mast with a single sail can be raised to make long voyages easier on the muscles of the passengers.

Tepuke

The tepuke is a very fast ship with two parallel hulls and a single sail on the single mast. (OOC: In real life it is the long voyage canoe of the Polynesians.)

Longboat

The typical vessel of the vikings. The simple ship is surprisingly fast. It has a single sail on its single mast and can be rowed to increase speed or move against the wind. Traditionally a carving of a dragons head proudly sits on bow and stern.

Sloop

Sloops are usually small, single masted craft, carrying a huge spread of sail in proportion to their size. This makes them fast and manoeuvrable, and their shallow draft and fast lines simply look good. Their sail arrangement is normally a fore-and-aft rig, with a mainsail and a single foresail.

Cutter

The cutter is a fast small single masted vessel with a fore-and-aft mainsail, a foresail and a jib. It also frequently carries a square-rigged topsail.

Schooner

Schooners are small two masted ships carrying fore and aft sails on both masts. Their narrow hulls and large spread of sail makes them fast, capable of exceeding 11 knots in the right conditions. They also have a shallow draft. Because of their sturdy build and high speed they are valued as warships.

Pink

The pink is a small, flat-bottomed ship with a narrow stern. (OOC: The name derived from the Dutch word pincke.) They are generally square-rigged and used as merchantmen.

Tartan

The tartan is fast, manueverable, and narrow. She sports one mast with a lateen mainsail and a small foresail on her bowsprit. (OOC: The name applies to an Arabic ship used in the Mediterranean.)

Junk

The junk has a flat bottom with no keel, flat bow, and a high stern. A junk's width is about a third of its length and she has a rudder which can be lowered or raised providing excellent steering capabilities. A junk has two masts with square sails, made from bamboo, rattan or grass. Contrary to belief, the junk is capable of operating in any seas as she is a very sea-worthy vessel. (OOC: The word junk comes from the Javanese word djong, which means ship.)

Dhow

Dhows are meant to be trading ships. They have very much cargo capacity needing only few sailors to man the single mast which is lateen-rigged.

Brig

The brig is a fully square-rigged two-masted vessel, although it sometimes uses an additional fore-and-aft sail between the two masts. It is a very sturdy ship valued by the navies of Tarmellion because of her speed and hull space.

Snow

The snow has two mast with square-rigg sails and a gaff-rigged sail at the back of the mainmast. Admirals throughout the world prefer this ship because of its combination of fighting capabilities and sailing abilities.

Galley

The galley has one deck and is mainly powered by oars, but it also has a single mast with a single sail.

Caravel

The caravel is a two-masted ship with lateen-rigged sails.

Galleon

The galleon are large ships meant for transporting cargo. Galleons are sluggish behemoths, not able to sail into or near the wind. They have three masts, forward masts being square-rigged, lateen-sails on the mizzenmast, and a small square sail on her high-rising bowsprit.

Carrack

The carrack are 3 masted with square sails on the fore and main masts and lateen-rigged on the mizzen. They have very high fore and aft-castles.

Cog

The cog is the largest merchant ship of Tarmellion. It has three masts which are square-rigged.

Floating Fortress

The floating fortress is a speciality normally seen only on the underground waters. It is primarily a fighting platform with enforced boards that is propelled with lots of small oars.

War Galley

The war galley is a galley rebuild for use in sea wars. Additional oars and protection for the onboard soldiers are added.

War Galleon

The war galleon is a galleon build from ironwood that gives the ship and its crew additional protection. They have three masts, forward masts being square-rigged, lateen-sails on the mizzenmast, and a small square sail on her high-rising bowsprit.

Ghost Ship

The ghost ship is a magical vessel. It is a caravel whose two masts are handled by ghosts (hence the name) or other mystical creatures. It only needs one really good sailor to give the correct orders.
